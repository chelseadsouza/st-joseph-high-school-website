OUR VISION

We aim to touch the hearts & minds of the students confided to our care. To prepare the students grow sensitively to belong & build a society, nation and the world as one humanity of God. To ignite a spirit of virtuous living & help develop their talents to contribute towards peace, unity and justice.

OUR MISSION

The Mission of St. Joseph High School is

  • To impart a Human and Christian education, that is universally beneficial and effective as life skill.
  • To studiously and passionately give their utmost in curricular and co-curricular learning.
  • To inculcate in students areas of moral, intellectual, physical, social, aesthetic and emotional development. This process will take place in the environment conducive to learning - providing opportunities to have logical & Critical understanding.
  • To Encourage deep interest in cultures and curiosity in research and discovery to innovate and face contemporary challenges.
  • To Establish well founded values embedded in the constitution of our nation in practices.
  • To uphold dignity and Unity off all as one people and humanity respecting environment and habitat as a common home to all creatures.

OUR MOTTO

The motto of St Joseph High School is a minority school, aims at education, mainly for the catholic community around and to the extent possible to the members of all communities.

Our school stands for academic excellence, character formation, development of skills based on the love of god and service of man as modelled on Jesus Christ with a view to training students to be an ideal citizen for the society, nation and sincere commitment to god and community.

True education stands for all faculties physical, intellectual, emotional social, spiritual and moral aspects given due attention, main motto of our school reads‚Äù virtute et labore" which means " by means of virtue and hard work". The school wishes to bring out the best in the child by imparting and making them aware of importance of good character and hardwork.

The main aim is to reawaken in young children a critical knowledge of the richness of their own self and to know how to actively insert themselves into society, thus becoming an element of social change for a better tomorrow.

  1. Promote self-exploration, self-actualization and self-direction.

General Information

St. Joseph High School is a minority school and was founded and established by the Catholic Church in 1875. It is administered by St. Joseph's Church, Umerkhadi, a Roman Catholic Church. The school is under the religious jurisdiction of the Roman Catholic Archbishop of Mumbai. The high school is recognised and affiliated to the State Board of Education, the Government of Maharashtra and the Primary to the B.M.C.

School Organization

In the education of our students co-curricular and extra curricular activities plays a vital part. A particular aspect of these activities is participation of the students in the School Management. Thus the student learns under the supervision of a teacher to do things for themselves and thus to view the practical side of important but abstract ideas of duty, co-operation and responsibility.

The House System

In order to inculcate a healthy spirit of competition and to get the best out of each individual student St. Joseph high School's students are divided into four houses. Each House is named after an important personality. The Houses are divided as follows:
Gandhi House : Blue
Nehru House : Green
Tilak House: Yellow
Tagore House: Red

The School Council

It is a body established to put into practice the aims and objectives of the house System. The Council is made up of the Head Boy, Head Girl Asst. Head Boy Asst. Head Girl School Captains, School Vice Captains as well as the class Perfects and Monitors and Asst perfects and monitors and other representatives of the various activities and organisation. The Meetings of the council are conducted on a Parliamentary basis. They help the staff in carrying out various responsibilities in the school, especially discipline before schools starts during the recess and at the end of the school. The Council also sees to the cleanliness of the school and helps to maintain order at school functions.
